Weekly Assignments:

Dinner and Syllabus:

1.) Complete the College Student Inventory and Bystander Training (You have both links in an email).

2.) Read the syllabus carefully. Do you have questions? Write them and ask them during the next class session.



Vocation:

1.) Read "Vocation Needs No Justification" by Stephan Garber.

2.) Use the ideas from the reading and your vocation map to begin your final essay through this reflection: Write a 300-400 word refection that tries to answer the question: What is your current vocation?



Liberal Arts:

1.) Complete the reflection and bring it to your one-on-one with your professor.



One-On-One:

1.) What "You Choose" session would be most helpful to you? Which sessions do you plan to attend over the next two weeks?



Diversity and Inclusion:

1.) Take notes on the UnLearn week session you attend. Incorperate ideas from the session into your final essay.



Campus Resources:

1.) Take notes on the information session you attend. Incorperate ideas from the session into your final essay.

2.) Write your final essay.



Celebration and Reflection:

1.) Turn in your final essay.

2.) Celebrate with your classmates and share your reflections.



Final Essay Description:

What key themes emerge from these works?

How have the past seven weeks challenged and shaped you?

Format:

*600-800 word double spaced

*Times New Roman 12pt.font

*Printed and turned in on last day

*Graded on Rubic
